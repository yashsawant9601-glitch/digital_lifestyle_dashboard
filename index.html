Screen Time by State
Delhi leads with highest average daily screen time at 6.90 hrs. Screen time variation across states is relatively tight, suggesting nationwide digital adoption. Tier 1 cities in high-connectivity states dominate.
Income by Occupation (₹K)
Corporate and Healthcare professionals earn highest incomes (₹600K+), while Students and Unemployed earn significantly less. Tech workers sit in the middle tier at ~₹350K annually.
Gender Distribution
Males account for 57.8%, Females 41.1%, and Non-binary users 1.1%. The dataset captures diverse gender representation across Indian digital users.
Age vs Screen Time (hrs)
Screen time peaks sharply in teens and early 20s (7.5–7.9 hrs), then steadily declines with age. Users above 40 average under 5 hrs, showing a clear generational digital divide.
Sleep vs Stress Level
Strong negative correlation — users sleeping under 5 hrs consistently report stress levels of 8–10. Better sleep (7–9 hrs) aligns with significantly lower stress (2–4), confirming sleep as a critical wellness factor.
Screen Time vs Work-Life Balance
Higher screen time (10–18 hrs) is associated with lower work-life balance scores (2–5). Users with moderate screen time (4–8 hrs) report the best balance, suggesting a healthy digital usage zone.
App Usage Distribution
Social media dominates app usage at 36.7%, followed by Productivity (27.8%) and Gaming (20.6%). News consumption at 14.9% reflects India's growing digital news audience.

About This Dataset

The Indian Digital Lifestyle Dataset captures the digital behaviour and wellbeing patterns of 38,928 users spanning multiple Indian states, city tiers, age groups, and occupations. Collected to understand the intersection of technology use and personal health, it tracks daily screen time, social media consumption, app usage categories, sleep quality, stress indicators, physical activity, and work-life balance scores. This dataset enables analysis of how digital habits correlate with mental and physical wellbeing across diverse socioeconomic profiles in India — offering insights for policymakers, health researchers, and product teams focused on digital wellness interventions.
